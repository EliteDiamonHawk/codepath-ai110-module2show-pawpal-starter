## 2. Scheduling Logic and Tradeoffs

**a. Constraints and priorities**

- What constraints does your scheduler consider (for example: time, priority, preferences)?
- How did you decide which constraints mattered most?

I decided to have the schedule prioritize highest priority value, longer time, then non repeat (or less repeat) preference in that order. I thought priority mattered most as it is the user's choice/input. preference, that is, if is a one-day task, weekly task, or daily-task is prioritized in that order. This is because a task that occurs less can be assummed to be more special or important. Time is last since tasks can have a large amount of varing times and task times can often be inaccurate in practice. it can be assummed that tasks with largest time is more important or that the smaller timed tasks can be fit in outside of the strict scheduling.

**b. Tradeoffs**

- Describe one tradeoff your scheduler makes.
- Why is that tradeoff reasonable for this scenario?

My scheduler greedily picks tasks in priority order until time runs out. This means a single long, high-priority task can consume most of the available time, causing several shorter lower-priority tasks to be skipped — even though those tasks could have all fit together in less time than the high-priority one. This tradeoff is reasonable because the scheduler is built around the owner's stated priorities: if a task is marked high priority, it should be guaranteed a spot in the schedule.
